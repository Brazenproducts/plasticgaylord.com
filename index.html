Why Plastic Gaylords Outperform Cardboard in Food & Agriculture

Cardboard Gaylord boxes have been the bulk container default for decades — but they come with serious hidden costs. A single contamination event from a soggy cardboard wall, a food-safety audit that flags fibre contamination, or a compressed-and-collapsed Gaylord on a warehouse rack can wipe out the apparent cost savings instantly.

Polypropylene Gaylord boxes solve every one of those failure modes. PP is non-porous, so it never absorbs moisture, produce condensation, or cleaning chemicals. The walls maintain structural integrity at −30°C freezer temperatures and through repeat pressure-washing cycles. And at end of life, 100% of the PP material is recyclable into secondary PP products — no landfill waste.

Collapsible vs Rigid: Which Do You Need?

The biggest decision in selecting a plastic Gaylord is whether you need a collapsible or rigid wall design. Rigid Gaylords are ideal when the box stays in a closed loop — from your facility to a fixed customer and back. They offer the highest stacking strength (up to 4 loaded boxes high) and the lowest per-unit cost at scale.

Collapsible PP Gaylords are the answer when return freight cost is a concern. An empty collapsible Gaylord folds to approximately 25% of its loaded height, meaning you can return 4 boxes in the space of 1. For operations shipping Gaylords out regionally and recovering them by the pallet, the freight savings on returns typically pay back the price premium over rigid boxes within 6–8 months.

Food-Grade Certification: What to Ask For

Not all plastic Gaylord boxes are food-contact safe. The PP resin used must be virgin or food-contact certified recycled grade, free from plasticisers, heavy metal pigments, and processing additives that can migrate into food. Elipacko's food-grade Gaylord line is manufactured from NSF-listed PP resin and carries FDA 21 CFR compliance documentation. Request the material safety data sheet and migration test report before purchasing for any food, pharma, or nutraceutical application.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard Gaylord box size?

The industry-standard Gaylord footprint is 48×40 inches, matching a GMA (Grocery Manufacturers Association) standard pallet. Depth options typically range from 30 to 48 inches depending on product density and stack height requirements. Custom sizes are available from Elipacko with a minimum order quantity.

How many times can a plastic Gaylord be reused?

Under normal warehouse conditions — indoor use, standard loading, routine pressure-wash cleaning — a well-made PP Gaylord will complete 50 to 80+ trip cycles before showing structural degradation. This compares to a single-use cardboard Gaylord that typically cannot be reused at all in wet or cold-chain environments.

Are plastic Gaylords food safe?

Yes, when manufactured from FDA 21 CFR compliant PP resin. Always request the material compliance documentation from your supplier. Elipacko provides full traceability documentation for food-grade Gaylord orders.

Can plastic Gaylords be used with pallet racks?

Yes. The 48×40 footprint fits standard selective racking. For racking applications, specify your beam span and load requirement — some Gaylord designs require a base board or pallet to distribute load across rack beams. Elipacko's heavy-duty models include an integrated skid base for direct racking.
